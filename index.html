India's Economy and Trade

India is one of the world's fastest-growing major economies and a key player in global trade. With a GDP of over $3.7 trillion (nominal, 2024), it ranks as the fifth-largest economy after the US, China, Germany, and Japan. India's economic growth is driven by a mix of services, manufacturing, and agriculture, along with strong domestic consumption and increasing foreign investments.

Services and Goods Imports

Over five decades, India's goods trade grew more than 300 times, while services, since 1991, grew nearly 50 times, underscoring the nation's transition into a strong global trade player, particularly in IT and service exports.

Top Goods Imports

India's top 5 imports are led by petroleum (32.30%) in goods, followed by electrical machinery, gems & jewellery, mechanical machinery, and organic chemicals. In services, business services (33.28%) dominate, with significant shares from travel, transportation, software services, and financial services.

India Export Goods And Services Yearly Trends

India's exports have shown strong long-term growth from $0 to $437B in goods and $341B in services in 2024. Goods exports expanded steadily, with major surges after 2000. Services exports grew rapidly with the rise of IT, crossing $300B in 2023.

India Top 5 Exports In 2025

India's top exports are led by petroleum (20.04%) in goods, followed by electrical machinery, gems & jewellery. In services, software services dominate with 47.01%, ahead of business services and transportation.

India Services Export Over Time

From 2001 to 2024, India's services exports grew sharply, with software services surging from $17.7B to over $160B, making it the dominant sector. Business services crossed $88B in 2024.

India Trade Partners

Major export destinations: US ($77.5B), UAE ($35.6B). Major import sources: China ($101.7B), Russia ($61.2B). The US and UAE lead in exports, while China dominates imports.
